Tao Lei

I am currently a scientist and a research leader at ASAPP where I manage a group of researchers and engineers for natural language processing (NLP) and applied machine learning. Prior to joining ASAPP, I completed my PhD at Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) in 2017. I was advised by Prof. Regina Barzilay and worked in the NLP group at CSAIL. I obtained my bachelor of science degree from Peking University in 2010.

Broadly speaking, I am interested in the algorithmic perspective of machine learning and various applications of natural language processing. My recent research focuses on the following topics:

  • Efficient ML: devise neural network models that can scale at training or inference while retaining state-of-the-art results on target applications (Preprint, EMNLP18, ICML17); and build novel learning algorithms for model compression (EMNLP20a, EMNLP20b).

  • Rationalizing model prediction: introduce self-explaining models that selects a subset of input as a justification for the models prediction (EMNLP16, ACL20a); or thru interacting with the user (ACL20b).
